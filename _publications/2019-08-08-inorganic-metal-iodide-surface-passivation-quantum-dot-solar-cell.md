Solution-phase ligand exchange has enabled production-compatible quantum dot (QD) inks for large-area deposition in solar cell applications. Existing strategies using inorganic–organic hybrid halometallate ligands achieve passivation and colloidal stability but retain organic cations, which can compromise device stability. In this work, we develop a fully inorganic ligand exchange route using alkali metal halides (e.g., CsI, NaI) as a substitute for the organic counter-cation. The inorganic halometallate anions bind to unsaturated metal sites on the PbS QD surface, providing passivation, while the alkali cations maintain colloidal stability and charge balance. The resulting QD inks are compatible with single-coating large-area deposition, and photovoltaic devices fabricated from these inks show competitive performance, with improved potential for long-term stability.
