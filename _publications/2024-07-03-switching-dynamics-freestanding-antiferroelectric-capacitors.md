Antiferroelectric materials such as lead zirconate (PbZrO3) are of great interest for high energy-storage density capacitors and solid-state cooling applications via the negative electrocaloric effect. In this work, we fabricate antiferroelectric capacitors of PbZrO3 (300 nm) sandwiched between SrRuO3 electrodes and release them from the growth substrate to create freestanding membranes. Comparing freestanding and epitaxially-clamped capacitors, we find that declamping leads to lower dispersion of switching fields, lower residual polarization, and faster switching response. As a result, the freestanding capacitors exhibit superior energy storage density and efficiency, highlighting the importance of substrate-induced mechanical boundary conditions for antiferroelectric device performance.
