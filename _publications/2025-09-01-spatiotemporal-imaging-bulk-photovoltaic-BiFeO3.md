The bulk photovoltaic effect (BPVE) in ferroelectrics arises directly from the broken inversion symmetry of the crystal structure, yet its microscopic charge transport mechanism and intrinsic efficiency have remained elusive. In this work, we employ ultrafast spatiotemporal microscopy to directly image the spatial transport of photoexcited carriers in multiferroic BiFeO3 on sub-nanosecond timescales. We find that the direction of ferroelectric polarization dictates an asymmetric drift of electrons along the polar axis, and that the BPVE is intrinsically a highly efficient process — far exceeding the macroscopic efficiencies typically reported for devices. These results provide direct microscopic evidence for the BPVE mechanism and point toward strategies for engineering more efficient photo-ferroic devices.
