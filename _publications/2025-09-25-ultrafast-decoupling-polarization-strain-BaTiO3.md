At equilibrium, the ferroelectric polarization is proportional to the strain, and this coupling is typically assumed to persist out of equilibrium as well. In this work, we use above-bandgap laser excitation of the prototypical ferroelectric BaTiO3 and directly observe an ultrafast decoupling between polarization and strain. The decoupling begins within 350 fs via charge-transfer-induced softening of Ti-O bonds and persists for several tens of picoseconds. This out-of-equilibrium state, where polarization is primarily determined by photoexcited electrons rather than by strain, provides an effective optical knob to manipulate ferroelectric polarization and opens new design possibilities for optically controlled ferroelectric devices.
